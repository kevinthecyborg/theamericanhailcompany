Is it worth claiming hail damage to my car?

Yes, it's absolutely worthwhile. Hail damage falls under comprehensive coverage, which is considered an "act of God" and won't count against you or raise your rates. Repairing hail damage helps maintain your vehicle's value. If your vehicle is leased or financed, you're typically required to have full coverage insurance.

How much does hail damage repair cost?

Repair costs vary depending on the extent of damage. Typically, it ranges from $70-$100 per dent, with larger dents costing up to $150. At The American Hail Company, we use skilled technicians and advanced techniques to restore your vehicle without compromising its integrity.

How is hail damage repaired?

The preferred method is Paintless Dent Repair (PDR). Our technicians use specialized tools to carefully massage the exterior back to its original form. This method preserves your vehicle's factory finish and value better than traditional bodywork, which may use fillers or aftermarket parts.

How can repairs be "free"?

While not truly free, there's no out-of-pocket cost to you. Your insurance pays for the repairs, and we waive your deductible. You're already paying for insurance monthly, so using it for a comprehensive claim like hail damage makes financial sense.

Can I choose my own repair facility?

Yes, as the policyholder, you have the legal right to choose your repair shop. We recommend choosing carefully, as some shops may use lower-quality parts or methods. At The American Hail Company, we use only OEM parts from local dealers and manufacturers.

Should I file a claim and keep the money instead of repairing my car?

We strongly advise against this. Insurance companies may deny future claims due to pre-existing damage, as they can't differentiate between hail damage from different storms. It's best to have the damage repaired properly.
